# Miniature Wargame Japanese Terminology Conventions

Japanese miniature wargame rulebooks draw on three vocabulary layers: classical military Japanese (手番, 突撃, 行軍), post-WWII wargame documentation (JWC board game glossary), and katakana loanwords (プレイヤー, フェイズ). The balance among these layers distinguishes a game's register and influences its perceived genre affiliation.

## Phase terminology

The most commercially sensitive choice is the phase-word. フェイズ is a generic loanword, but the specific compounds 突撃フェイズ, 射撃フェイズ, 白兵戦フェイズ, and 移動フェイズ are named game terms in Warhammer 40,000 and Age of Sigmar Japan rulebooks. Projects independent of Games Workshop should use 段階 (dankai) as the phase-word: 突撃段階, 射撃段階, 白兵戦段階, 移動段階.

## Stat terminology

Japanese miniature wargame stat profiles use a standard set of concepts: 体力 (HP), 移動 (movement), 攻撃力 (attack power), 防御 (defense), 耐久力 (toughness/resilience), 攻撃数 (number of attacks), 貫通力 (armour penetration), 士気 (morale/discipline). WH Japan abbreviates these with single kanji (攻, 防, 傷, etc.) on datasheets. Full-word stat names are safe; WH's single-kanji abbreviation convention should be avoided.

## Cover and terrain

遮蔽 (cover) is a classical Japanese term and safe to use, though WH Japan does use 遮蔽物ボーナス (cover bonus). The neutral alternative is 障害 (obstacle). 地形 (terrain type/rules) is safe; テレイン is WH Japan's term for physical terrain model pieces.

## HP mechanic distinction

WH Japan tracks HP using 傷 (wounds), counting wounds taken rather than a remaining total. Other Japanese wargames use 体力 (health points, a depleting total). The distinction matters for rulebook prose: use 体力 for standard HP mechanics.
