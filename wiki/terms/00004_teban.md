# 手番 (teban)

**turn / one's turn to act** · てばん · teban

one's turn to act; the right and responsibility to move

## Orthography notes

Native Japanese compound: 手 (hand / move) + 番 (turn / one's place in sequence). The shogi/go origin predates all modern miniature wargaming. ターン Wikipedia Japan (ターン (ゲーム)) explicitly traces the origin of the 手番 concept to shogi.

## Implicit meanings

手番 frames the turn as obligation as well as right — it is whose task it is to move, not merely whose opportunity.

## Differences across game settings

In shogi and go, 手番 refers specifically to the right to move next. In board and miniature wargames, the meaning extends to the full scope of a player's action phase within a round.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤー (active player); {手|て}{番|ばん}を{交|か}わす (pass the turn); あなたの{手|て}{番|ばん}です (it is your turn)
**English:** "turn" as a noun; sometimes "your move" in contexts derived from abstract strategy games.

## Comparative notes

ターン (taan) is the dominant loanword in video games and many modern board games. 手番 is preferred in contexts emphasizing the Japanese game-design lineage or for explicit shogi/go cross-reference. Both are IP-safe.

## Examples

> {手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤーは{行|こう}{動|どう}を{実|じっ}{行|こう}する。
> The active player executes an action.
