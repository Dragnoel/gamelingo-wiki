# 敏捷性 (binshousei)

**agility** · びんしょうせい · binshousei

a stat representing quickness, dexterity, or reflexes; may affect initiative, dodge, or movement

## Orthography notes

敏捷: 敏 (quick/nimble) + 捷 (agile/swift). 性 (sei) as a suffix: quality/nature. 捷 (U+6377) is a less-common kanji but standard in 敏捷. Goo dictionary lists 敏捷 as a general adjective. Hobby Japan D&D translation uses 敏捷性 for Dexterity. Not WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

敏捷性 is the established Japanese TRPG rendering of Dexterity (D&D, Pathfinder). The docx notes that 速度 (speed) is incorrect for this concept — 速度 means rate of motion, not reflexive quickness.

## Differences across game settings

TRPG-standard. Hobby Japan D&D and Pathfinder translations both use 敏捷性 for the Dexterity/Agility ability score. In wargames, used for initiative and evasion stats.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {敏|びん}{捷|しょう}{性|せい}ステータス (Agility stat); {敏|びん}{捷|しょう}{性|せい}判{定|てい} (Agility check, TRPG)
**English:** "Agility", "Dexterity", "DEX stat".

## Comparative notes

速度 (sokudo, speed) is incorrect — it measures rate of motion, not reflexive quickness. 敏捷性 is the established TRPG standard for Dexterity/Agility.

## Examples

> {敏|びん}{捷|しょう}{性|せい}が{高|たか}いユニットは{先|せん}{攻|こう}できる。
> High-Agility units may act first.
