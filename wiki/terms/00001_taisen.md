# 対戦 (taisen)

**match / competitive bout** · たいせん · taisen

a competitive match or bout between two players or sides

## Orthography notes

Always written in kanji (対戦). No katakana variant in standard usage. The compound uses 対 (facing / opposing) + 戦 (war / battle).

## Implicit meanings

Implies adversarial structure; used even when the game has cooperative elements if sides are opposed.

## Differences across game settings

Universal across board games, video games, and TCGs. In miniature wargames, frames the entire game session as a single match. JWC wargame glossary uses 対戦 as the default term for a game-length encounter.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {対|たい}{戦|せん}する (to play a match); {対|たい}{戦|せん}{相|あい}{手|て} (opponent); {対|たい}{戦|せん}{表|ひょう} (match record / pairing sheet)
**English:** "match" in competitive contexts; "game" when referring to a single session.

## Comparative notes

Preferred over 試合 (shiai) in board and miniature wargame contexts, where 試合 reads as sports-specific. 対局 (taikyoku) is the shogi/go equivalent but is not used outside those domains.

## Examples

> {対|たい}{戦|せん}{相|あい}{手|て}を{甘|あま}く{見|み}るな。
> Don't underestimate your opponent.
