# 体力 (tairyoku)

**health points / stamina** · たいりょく · tairyoku

a unit's total health points; the pool of vitality that is depleted by damage

## Orthography notes

体力: 体 (body) + 力 (power/capacity). Weblio dictionary lists it as a general word. Used universally in Japanese games. WH Japan's 傷 (wound) mechanic is distinct and WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

体力 is the universal Japanese game term for HP, used for decades across all game genres. WH Japan uses 傷 (wounds) — a tracking-wounds-taken mechanic that should not be confused with a health-point total.

## Differences across game settings

Universal across all Japanese game genres. The docx explicitly notes: WH Japan uses 傷 (wounds) for HP — this is WH-specific and should be avoided. 体力 is the safe, universal term.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {体|たい}{力|りょく}が0になると{ユニット|ゆにっと}は{除|じょ}{去|きょ}される (when HP reaches 0, the unit is removed); {体|たい}{力|りょく}ゲージ (HP bar)
**English:** "health points", "HP", "stamina".

## Comparative notes

傷 (kizu, wound) is WH Japan's tracking mechanic and must be avoided in non-WH contexts. 体力 is the standard neutral Japanese HP term.

## Examples

> {体|たい}{力|りょく}が0になったユニットは{戦|せん}{場|じょう}から{除|じょ}{去|きょ}される。
> Units whose HP reaches 0 are removed from the battlefield.
