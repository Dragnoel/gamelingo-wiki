# 士気 (shiki)

**discipline / morale** · しき · shiki

a morale or discipline stat representing a unit's willingness to keep fighting under pressure

## Orthography notes

士気: 士 (warrior/soldier) + 気 (spirit/energy). Classical Japanese military term for morale. Goo dictionary. JWC wargame glossary attests 士気 as the standard morale term. WH Japan uses 戦意フェイズ (battle-spirit phase) for the morale phase — 士気 is the underlying concept and not a WH term.

## Implicit meanings

士気 is a classical Japanese military concept directly translating the military idea of troop morale or esprit de corps. Used in JWC documentation consistently.

## Differences across game settings

Standard in board wargames and historical miniature wargames. JWC documentation uses 士気 for morale checks. WH Japan's 戦意フェイズ is a WH-specific phase label; 士気 itself is classical and independent.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {士|し}{気|き}チェック (morale check); {士|し}{気|き}が{崩|くず}れる (morale breaks); {士|し}{気|き}テスト (discipline test)
**English:** "discipline", "morale", "morale check".

## Comparative notes

戦意フェイズ (WH Japan) uses 戦意 (battle will) as the morale concept; 士気 is the classical general term. 士気 is safe for all non-WH uses.

## Examples

> でも、それで{士|し}{気|き}が{下|さ}がっては{本|ほん}{末|まつ}{転|てん}{倒|とう}ではないかと。
> But, if by that, morale drops, wouldn't that defeat the purpose?
