# プレイヤー (pureiyaa)

**player** · プレイヤー · pureiyaa

a person taking part in a game; the human decision-maker behind a side

## Orthography notes

Katakana loanword from English 'player'. No standard kanji form exists in the gaming sense. The docx notes 選手 (athlete) as an inappropriate substitute.

## Implicit meanings

In miniature wargames, プレイヤー refers to the person, not their army. Rulebooks use it to address the reader directly.

## Differences across game settings

Universal across all Japanese game genres. Warhammer Japan also uses it but did not originate the term. Fire Emblem (Nintendo, 1990–present) and Famicom Wars (Nintendo, 1988–present) use it independently.

## Usage

**Japanese:** プレイヤーは{手|て}{番|ばん}に{行|こう}{動|どう}する (the player acts on their turn); {手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤー (active player)
**English:** "the player" or "you" when rulebooks address the reader.

## Comparative notes

選手 is the correct word in sports contexts but reads as too competitive and physical for tabletop usage. プレイヤー is the universally accepted loanword.

## Examples

> {手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤーが{行|こう}{動|どう}を{選|えら}ぶ。
> The active player chooses an action.
