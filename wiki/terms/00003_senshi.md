# 戦士 (senshi)

**warrior / fighter unit** · せんし · senshi

a warrior or combat-capable unit type

## Orthography notes

Native Japanese compound: 戦 (war/battle) + 士 (person of skill/rank). Not a Warhammer coinage; predates modern miniature wargames. Kotobank and Weblio list it as a general dictionary term.

## Implicit meanings

Carries connotations of melee or close-combat specialization. Does not imply rank or command.

## Differences across game settings

Broad game-setting applicability. In TRPGs (D&D Japanese translation, Hobby Japan), 戦士 is a character class name. In miniature wargames, used as a unit type. 武者 is preferred in games with explicitly Japanese historical settings.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {戦|せん}{士|し}ユニット (warrior unit); {戦|せん}{士|し}クラス (warrior class)
**English:** "warrior", "fighter", or "soldier" depending on context. Use "warrior" when heroic or classlike; "soldier" when military-regimented.

## Comparative notes

武者 (musha) is the classical samurai-era cognate but is domain-specific. 兵士 (heishi, soldier) implies military rank structure and is less common as a unit type name.

## Examples

> {戦|せん}{士|し}が{戦|たたか}う。
> Warriors fight.
