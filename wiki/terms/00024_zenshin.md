# 前進 (zenshin)

**advance** · ぜんしん · zenshin

a movement action in which a unit moves forward toward the enemy

## Orthography notes

前進: 前 (forward/front) + 進 (advance/proceed). Goo dictionary military definition. JWC wargame movement documentation attests 前進 as a standard movement term. WH Japan uses 全力移動 for the Advance action — 前進 is therefore independent.

## Implicit meanings

前進 is a classical Japanese military command meaning 'forward march' or 'advance'. It carries the connotation of deliberate, goal-directed movement toward the enemy rather than repositioning.

## Differences across game settings

In miniature wargames, 前進 is typically a shorter advance move (as opposed to 行軍, which allows more distance but restricts other actions). Occurs in the 移動段階.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {前|ぜん}{進|しん}する (to advance); {前|ぜん}{進|しん}を{宣|せん}{言|げん}する (declare an advance)
**English:** "advance", "move forward".

## Comparative notes

行軍 (kougun, march) implies longer strategic movement at the cost of other actions. 前進 is the shorter tactical advance. WH Japan uses 全力移動 for a comparable action, so 前進 is an independent term.

## Examples

> {彼|かれ}らは{川|かわ}まで{前|ぜん}{進|しん}した。
> They advanced to the river.
