# 射線 (shasen)

**line of sight** · しゃせん · shasen

the unobstructed path between an attacker and a target that determines whether an attack can be made

## Orthography notes

射線: 射 (shoot/fire) + 線 (line). Goo dictionary defines it as a military term for 'firing line'. WH Japan uses 視認 (visual recognition) for its LOS concept. 射線 appears independently in Japanese military contexts.

## Implicit meanings

射線 is a military ballistics term for the firing line or line of fire. The docx notes that the current (unspecified) alternative 視覚の線 is an unnatural calque of English 'line of sight' and should be replaced.

## Differences across game settings

In wargames, 射線が通る (line of sight is clear) vs. 射線が遮られる (line of sight is blocked). Not WH-specific.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {射|しゃ}{線|せん}が{通|とお}る (line of sight is clear); {射|しゃ}{線|せん}が{遮|さえぎ}られる (line of sight is blocked); {射|しゃ}{線|せん}を{確|かく}{認|にん}する (check line of sight)
**English:** "line of sight" or "firing line".

## Comparative notes

視覚の線 is an unnatural calque and should be avoided. 視認範囲 is WH Japan's term. 射線 is the recommended classical Japanese military term.

## Examples

> {射|しゃ}{線|せん}が{地|ち}{形|けい}に{遮|さえぎ}られている。
> The line of sight is blocked by terrain.
