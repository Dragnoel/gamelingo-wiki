# 戦場 (senjou)

**battlefield** · せんじょう · senjou

the playing surface representing the battlefield; the zone of play

## Orthography notes

戦場: 戦 (battle) + 場 (place/field). Classical Japanese military and literary term. Attested in Goo dictionary as a general word. JWC documentation uses 戦場 consistently. No WH-specific concern.

## Implicit meanings

戦場 frames the entire game area as a single field of battle. It is a classical Japanese literary and military term, carrying historical and dramatic weight.

## Differences across game settings

Universal in wargame contexts. The docx notes this term is already in use and has classical Japanese military precedent with no Warhammer connection.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {戦|せん}{場|じょう}に{ユニット|ゆにっと}を{配|はい}{置|ち}する (deploy units on the battlefield); {戦|せん}{場|じょう}の{中|ちゅう}{央|おう} (center of the battlefield)
**English:** "battlefield", "battle map", or "playing field".

## Comparative notes

戦場 is the standard term for the playing surface in Japanese wargame documentation (JWC). No alternative is required.

## Examples

> {戦|せん}{場|じょう}の{中|ちゅう}{央|おう}を{制|せい}{圧|あつ}する。
> Control the center of the battlefield.
