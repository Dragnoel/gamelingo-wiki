# 加護 (kago)

**aegis / divine protection** · かご · kago

divine protection or a magical ward; a saving mechanic granting resistance to certain damage types

## Orthography notes

加護: 加 (add/grant) + 護 (protect). A classical Japanese Buddhist and Shinto term. Kotobank dictionary cites it as 'divine protection'. Used in Japanese RPGs broadly (JRPGs). Not WH-specific. アイギス is the Greek-mythology katakana alternative.

## Implicit meanings

加護 is a deeply Japanese Shinto and Buddhist concept meaning divine blessing/protection (as in 神の加護, 'divine grace'). Using 加護 for an aegis-type game mechanic grounds the game in Japanese spiritual vocabulary rather than Greek mythology.

## Differences across game settings

TRPGs and fantasy board games. 加護 gives the mechanic a Japanese spiritual flavour. アイギス (Greek: aegis, divine shield) is used in games explicitly referencing Greek mythology (Persona 3).

## Usage

**Japanese:** {神|かみ}の{加|か}{護|ご} (divine protection); {加|か}{護|ご}セービング (aegis saving roll)
**English:** "aegis", "divine protection", "ward".

## Comparative notes

加護 carries Japanese spiritual resonance. アイギス is appropriate for Greek-mythology-framed games. Neither is WH-specific.

## Examples

> {神|かみ}のご{加|か}{護|ご}がありますように。
> May God keep you!
