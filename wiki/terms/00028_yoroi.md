# 鎧 (yoroi)

**armour** · よろい · yoroi

armour worn by a unit; a protective equipment type that reduces damage taken

## Orthography notes

鎧 is a native Japanese word for traditional Japanese armour (鎧兜 = armour and helmet). Kotobank dictionary. WH Japan uses アーマーセーブ (armour save) as the saving mechanic term — 鎧 itself is not a WH term. 装甲 is the modern military term for vehicle armour.

## Implicit meanings

鎧 is a classical Japanese word for samurai armour (specifically lamellar/ōyoroi). In game contexts, it generalises to any body armour. The cultural resonance is strong.

## Differences across game settings

In fantasy wargames and TRPGs, 鎧 is the standard armour equipment term. In historical miniature wargames, 装甲 is preferred for mechanised/vehicle units.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {鎧|よろい}を{装|そう}{備|び}する (to equip armour); {鎧|よろい}の{防|ぼう}{御|ぎょ}{値|ち} (armour's defense value)
**English:** "armour" (British spelling standard in this project).

## Comparative notes

アーマーセーブ is WH Japan's specific saving mechanic term — avoid this compound. 鎧 refers to the armour itself and is classical Japanese with no WH concern. 装甲 is preferred for mechanised units.
