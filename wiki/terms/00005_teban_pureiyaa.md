# 手番プレイヤー (teban_pureiyaa)

**active player** · てばんプレイヤー · teban_pureiyaa

the player whose turn it currently is; the active player

## Orthography notes

Compound of native Japanese 手番 + katakana loanword プレイヤー. The mixed script is conventional in modern Japanese game rulebooks. No pure-kanji or pure-katakana equivalent is established.

## Implicit meanings

The compound foregrounds whose obligation it is to act, not merely whose right. Contrasts structurally with 非手番プレイヤー.

## Differences across game settings

Primarily board game and card game usage. Wikipedia Japan confirms ターンプレイヤー as a standard board game term; 手番プレイヤー is the preferred native-compound form.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤーは～を{行|おこな}う (the active player performs ～)
**English:** "active player" is the standard English equivalent in rulebook prose.

## Comparative notes

ターンプレイヤー is equally acceptable and may be preferred in contexts using ターン rather than 手番 as the turn-word throughout.

## Examples

> {手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤーは{移|い}{動|どう}{段|だん}{階|かい}を{開|かい}{始|し}する。
> The active player begins the moving phase.
