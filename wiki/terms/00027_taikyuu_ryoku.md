# 耐久力 (taikyuu_ryoku)

**resilience / toughness** · たいきゅうりょく · taikyuu_ryoku

a stat representing a unit's capacity to withstand damage; toughness

## Orthography notes

耐久: 耐 (endure) + 久 (lasting). 力 (ryoku) as a stat suffix: power/capacity. The compound 耐久力 is used in D&D Japanese translation (Hobby Japan) and across Japanese video games. Not WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

耐久力 represents passive durability, not active defensive action. In TRPG terms, it is closer to Constitution than Armor Class.

## Differences across game settings

Cross-genre applicable. Hobby Japan's D&D translation uses 耐久力 in stat terminology. Used across Japanese SRPGs and wargames for toughness stats.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {耐|たい}{久|きゅう}{力|りょく}ステータス (resilience stat); {耐|たい}{久|きゅう}{力|りょく}が{高|たか}いユニット (high-toughness unit)
**English:** "resilience", "toughness", "durability".

## Comparative notes

防御 (defense) implies active resistance or an armor-like modifier. 耐久力 is passive — it represents how tough a unit is constitutionally.

## Examples

> １{万|まん}メートルを{走|はし}るには{大|おお}いに{耐|たい}{久|きゅう}{力|りょく}を{必|ひつ}{要|よう}とする。
> You need great endurance to run ten thousand meters.
