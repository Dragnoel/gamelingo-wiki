# 行軍 (kougun)

**march** · こうぐん · kougun

an extended movement action that allows a unit to move at greater distance at the cost of other actions

## Orthography notes

行軍: 行 (go/march) + 軍 (military unit/army). Classical Japanese military term documented in WWII-era writing. JWC board wargame glossary attests 行軍 as a standard term. Goo dictionary defines it as a general military word.

## Implicit meanings

行軍 is a classical Japanese military term for troop movement over extended distances. The connotation is strategic movement (getting from A to B) rather than tactical engagement.

## Differences across game settings

In miniature wargames, 行軍 typically allows a unit to move farther (e.g., double movement distance) but prevents charging or shooting in the same turn. Occurs in the 移動段階.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {行|こう}{軍|ぐん}する (to march); {行|こう}{軍|ぐん}した{ユニット|ゆにっと}は{射|しゃ}{撃|げき}できない (units that marched cannot shoot)
**English:** "march", "force march".

## Comparative notes

前進 (advance) is the shorter tactical movement. 行軍 is the extended strategic movement with associated restrictions.

## Examples

> {行|こう}{軍|ぐん}したユニットは{射|しゃ}{撃|げき}{段|だん}{階|かい}に{攻|こう}{撃|げき}できない。
> Units that marched cannot attack during the shooting phase.
