# 防御 (bougyo)

**defense** · ぼうぎょ · bougyo

a unit's capacity to resist or absorb attacks; a defensive stat or action

## Orthography notes

防御: 防 (prevent/defend) + 御 (control/ward off). Standard Japanese military and game term. Goo dictionary entry is general. JWC glossary attests 防御. Not WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

防御 is the universal Japanese game and military term for defense. WH Japan abbreviates it as 防 on stat sheets, but the full word is entirely generic.

## Differences across game settings

Universal across all Japanese game genres. In miniature wargames, may be a numeric stat (防御値) or a saving throw modifier. Contrasts with 攻撃力 (attack power).

## Usage

**Japanese:** {防|ぼう}{御|ぎょ}ステータス (defense stat); {防|ぼう}{御|ぎょ}する (to defend); {防|ぼう}{御|ぎょ}{値|ち} (defense value)
**English:** "defense", "defensive stat", "armor rating".

## Comparative notes

攻撃力 (kougeki-ryoku) is the antonym in stat contexts. 耐久力 (resilience) covers a different concept — durability/toughness rather than active defense.

## Examples

> {敵|てき}の{攻|こう}{撃|げき}{力|りょく}が{自|じ}{分|ぶん}の{防|ぼう}{御|ぎょ}を{上|うわ}{回|まわ}る。
> The enemy's attack power exceeds my defense.
