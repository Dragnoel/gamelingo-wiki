# 高地 (kouchi)

**elevated ground / high ground** · こうち · kouchi

terrain that is elevated above the surrounding area, granting advantages to units occupying it

## Orthography notes

高地: 高 (high/tall) + 地 (ground/land). Goo dictionary lists it as a standard word meaning 'elevated land'. JWC terrain documentation attests it. Not WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

高地 is a classical Japanese military geography term extensively documented in WWII-era military writing. The phrase '高地を制する' (control the high ground) is a classical military maxim.

## Differences across game settings

Standard wargame terrain type. Units occupying 高地 gain advantages specified in the terrain rules. No WH-specific concern.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {高|こう}{地|ち}を{占|せん}{領|りょう}する (occupy the high ground); {高|こう}{地|ち}の{ユニット|ゆにっと}は+1の{射|しゃ}{撃|げき}ボーナスを{得|え}る (units on high ground gain +1 to shooting)
**English:** "high ground", "elevated terrain", "elevated position".

## Comparative notes

No direct WH concern. Classical military term with broad applicability in wargame and historical contexts.

## Examples

> {彼|かれ}らはその{高|こう}{地|ち}を{敵|てき}{軍|ぐん}に{明|あ}け{渡|わた}した。
> They abandoned the hill to enemy forces.
