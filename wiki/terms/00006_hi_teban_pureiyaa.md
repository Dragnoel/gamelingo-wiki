# 非手番プレイヤー (hi_teban_pureiyaa)

**reactive player / non-active player** · ひてばんプレイヤー · hi_teban_pureiyaa

the player(s) whose turn it is not; the non-active or reactive player

## Orthography notes

Uses the classical negation prefix 非 (hi-). The compound is not attested as a standalone dictionary entry but is constructed from standard elements and cited as a standard form in Wikipedia Japan (ターン (ゲーム)).

## Implicit meanings

非 (non-) is a formal negation prefix; the compound is a logical antonym formed from 非 + 手番プレイヤー. Not specific to any game system.

## Differences across game settings

Used in rulebooks that explicitly name both roles. In two-player miniature wargames, refers to the opponent. In multiplayer games, refers to all players except the active player.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {非|ひ}{手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤーは{反|はん}{応|のう}できる (the non-active player may react)
**English:** "reactive player", "non-active player", or "your opponent" depending on context.

## Comparative notes

The 非手番 prefix construction is productive in formal Japanese but uncommon in casual speech. Rulebooks use it for precision when contrasting active and reactive roles.

## Examples

> {非|ひ}{手|て}{番|ばん}プレイヤーはこの{段|だん}{階|かい}に{反|はん}{応|のう}できない。
> The non-active player cannot respond during this phase.
