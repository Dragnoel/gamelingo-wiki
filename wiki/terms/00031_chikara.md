# 力 (chikara)

**strength** · ちから · chikara

a stat representing physical strength; determines carrying capacity, melee damage, or similar physical effects

## Orthography notes

力 is a single-character fundamental Japanese word meaning power, force, or strength. 筋力 (kinryoku) is the TRPG-specific compound: 筋 (muscle) + 力 (power), used in D&D Japanese translation for the Strength ability score. Both are fully generic.

## Implicit meanings

力 (chikara) is the most fundamental Japanese word for power/force. In game stats, it usually refers to raw physical strength as opposed to skill (技), speed (速度), or intelligence (知恵).

## Differences across game settings

In TRPGs: 筋力 (Strength ability score, D&D Hobby Japan translation). In wargames: 力 is a simpler, more archaic-flavored stat label. In JRPGs, 力 frequently appears as the ATK or STR stat shorthand.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {力|ちから}ステータス (Strength stat); {筋|きん}{力|りょく}判{定|てい} (Strength check, TRPG)
**English:** "Strength", "STR stat".

## Comparative notes

筋力 is the TRPG-standard translation of Strength (D&D). 力 is appropriate for simpler wargame stat profiles or flavour-text usage. Neither is WH-specific.

## Examples

> {力|ちから}が{高|たか}いユニットは{近|きん}{接|せつ}{攻|こう}{撃|げき}で{有|ゆう}{利|り}だ。
> High-Strength units have an advantage in melee attacks.
