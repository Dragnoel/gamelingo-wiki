# 実行する (jikkou_suru)

**to perform / to execute** · じっこうする · jikkou_suru

to perform or carry out a game action

## Orthography notes

実行: 実 (actual/real) + 行 (go/carry out). Standard Japanese verb. Weblio dictionary lists it as a general-purpose term used in both everyday and technical (software, game) contexts. Not WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

実行する emphasizes concrete execution rather than declaration or intent. In game rules, it marks the resolution step: a player 実行する (carries out) what they have declared.

## Differences across game settings

Cross-genre applicable. In wargame rulebooks: '○○を実行する' (execute ○○) is the standard phrasing for rule resolution. Distinct from 宣言する (declare/announce an action).

## Usage

**Japanese:** {行|こう}{動|どう}を{実|じっ}{行|こう}する (execute an action); {突|とつ}{撃|げき}を{実|じっ}{行|こう}する (execute a charge)
**English:** "perform", "execute", "carry out".

## Comparative notes

宣言する (declare) is used when a player announces intent; 実行する is used when the action is actually resolved. Both appear in Japanese wargame rulebooks.

## Examples

> {彼|かれ}は{計|けい}{画|かく}を{実|じっ}{行|こう}した。
> He carried out the plan.
