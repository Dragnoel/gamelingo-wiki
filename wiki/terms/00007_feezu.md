# フェイズ (feezu)

**phase** · フェイズ · feezu

a defined subdivision of a turn or round during which specific actions occur

## Orthography notes

Katakana loanword from English 'phase'. フェイズ alone is used independently in Fire Emblem Japan (自軍フェイズ, 敵軍フェイズ) and Famicom Wars (発動フェイズ), predating WH Japan usage. 段階 (native compound: stage/step) is the recommended neutral alternative for phase-type compounds in this wargame project.

## Implicit meanings

フェイズ alone is low-risk as IP (it is simply the English loanword 'phase'). The risk arises with specific compounds: 突撃フェイズ, 射撃フェイズ, 白兵戦フェイズ, 移動フェイズ are the exact named phase terms used in WH40K Japan and AoS Japan rulebooks.

## Differences across game settings

In video games (SRPGs): 自軍フェイズ (player phase) / 敵軍フェイズ (enemy phase) is the Fire Emblem standard. In miniature wargames: using 段階 instead of フェイズ in phase-type compounds avoids WH Japan terminology.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {自|じ}{軍|ぐん}フェイズ (player phase, Fire Emblem); {突|とつ}{撃|げき}{段|だん}{階|かい} (charging phase, recommended wargame form)
**English:** "phase" as a structural turn subdivision.

## Comparative notes

段階 (dankai) is the recommended substitute in miniature wargame compounds. 局面 (kyokumen) implies a broader situational shift and is less common as a phase-label word. See individual phase entries (00008–00011) for each phase type.

## Examples

> {自|じ}{軍|ぐん}フェイズに{移|い}{動|どう}する。
> Move during the player phase.
