# 位置 (ichi)

**position** · いち · ichi

the spatial location of a unit on the battlefield; a unit's position

## Orthography notes

位置: 位 (rank/place) + 置 (put/place). Standard dictionary word. JWC documentation and Fire Emblem both use 配置 for unit placement in the setup phase, and 位置 for positional state during play.

## Implicit meanings

位置 is a general-purpose Japanese word for location/position, not game-specific. 配置 (haichi) is the related concept of arrangement or deployment.

## Differences across game settings

In miniature wargames: 位置 refers to a unit's location at any given moment. 配置 is used specifically at the start of the game for deploying units. Both are generic and not WH-specific.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {ユニット|ゆにっと}の{位|い}{置|ち}を{確|かく}{認|にん}する (check a unit's position); {配|はい}{置|ち}する (to deploy/place)
**English:** "position" for state; "placement" or "deployment" for 配置.

## Comparative notes

配置 (haichi) implies active arrangement by a player. 位置 is the resulting state. Both are needed in a wargame rulebook.

## Examples

> {位|い}{置|ち}について、よーい、ドン！
> On your mark, get set, go!
