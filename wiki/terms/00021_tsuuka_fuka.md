# 通過不可 (tsuuka_fuka)

**impassable** · つうかふか · tsuuka_fuka

a terrain type or status indicating that movement through it is prohibited

## Orthography notes

通過: 通 (through/pass) + 過 (pass). 不可: 不 (non-) + 可 (possible/permitted). Standard negation compound. Weblio dictionary lists 通過 as a general-purpose word. JWC terrain rules use 通過不可. Not WH-specific.

## Implicit meanings

通過不可 is a standard Japanese negation compound (通過 + 不可). The same construction appears on traffic signs (通行不可 = no through traffic) and military maps.

## Differences across game settings

In miniature wargames, impassable terrain blocks movement entirely. Example types: walls, cliffs, deep water. Units cannot enter or cross the terrain piece.

## Usage

**Japanese:** {通|つう}{過|か}{不|ふ}{可|か}の{地|ち}{形|けい} (impassable terrain); この{地|ち}{形|けい}は{通|つう}{過|か}{不|ふ}{可|か}です (this terrain is impassable)
**English:** "impassable", "impassable terrain".

## Comparative notes

Contrasts with 困難地形 (difficult terrain) which imposes movement penalties rather than prohibition. 通行不可 is a close synonym used on traffic signs; 通過不可 is the wargame-standard form.

## Examples

> {通|つう}{過|か}{不|ふ}{可|か}の{地|ち}{形|けい}には{侵|しん}{入|にゅう}できない。
> Units cannot enter impassable terrain.
