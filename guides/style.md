# Style guide — Gaming Language Wiki

## Voice and register
- Third person, present tense. Encyclopedic. No metaphor, rhetorical questions, second person.
- No hedging fluff. State the claim.
- No first-person voice, ever, including in comparative notes.

## Length norms
- Entity pages: 200–800 words.
- Lead paragraph: ≤ 80 words.
- Examples section: only examples sourced from raw/; no invented examples.

## Formatting
- ATX headers (#, ##) only. Wikilinks [[Title Case]] always.
- Footnote citations [^key] for every non-trivial claim.
- No tables unless data is genuinely tabular (not just a list).

## Japanese text
- Furigana notation {kanji|reading} in all JSON fields and in wiki pages where furigana is meaningful.
- Plain kanji or kana (no furigana) in running prose where the reading is established.
- Do not mix furigana notation and plain text in the same field.

## Prohibitions
- No bolded "key takeaway" callouts.
- No "Conclusion" headers.
- No emojis.
- No "according to recent studies" without naming the study.
- No "It is worth noting that…", "Interestingly,", or similar throat-clearing.
- No speculation about intent or player psychology without a sourced claim.
- No "TL;DR" summaries.
